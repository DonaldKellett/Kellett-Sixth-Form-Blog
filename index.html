New Feature: Smiley Face Character Recognition

I am glad to announce that as of 03/10/15, this blog is able to recognise certain text patterns as smiley faces and convert the text equivalent to actual smiley faces :)! That's right, you just saw a smiley face in my previous sentence!

Currently, this blog can identify and convert 4 smiley faces:

  • : + ) = :)
  • : + D = :D
  • : + p = :p
  • ; + ) = ;)

So from now on, for the commenting and blogging requests, apart from HTML format, smiley face characters are also supported! How cool is that? ;) Feel free to leave comments below! :D

As of 03/10/15, two new smiley faces have been added:

  • : + ( = :(
  • : + o = :o

Enjoy! :D

Initial Post

Hello Year 12 and Year 13 Youde,

Welcome to our new blog! In this blog, Year 13s will be able to make blog posts about their university applications and Year 12s will be able to comment on their posts. But before we start, please allow me to explain the system clearly.

As you should all know, this is a private blog so you will have to login to view the content of the blog. If you have not logged in to your account but can see this post, you are HACKING this blog (which of course is illegal). After you log in to your account, if you hover your mouse pointer over the "Menu" bar, depending on your rank (details later), you should see some (or all if your rank is high enough) of these options:

  • Home
  • View Profile
  • Request Comment
  • Request Post
  • Moderator Panel

It should be obvious that the "Home" button returns you to this blog page. As for the "View Profile" page, if you click on it, your profile details will appear, such as your blog name, your profile picture (if you don't have one, it defaults to the anonymous profile picture) and your rank in the blog. Sensitive details such as your blog username and password will not appear for obvious reasons.

Now, moving on to blog ranks. There are a total of 8 ranks among the users of this blog:

  1. Banned
  2. Visitor
  3. Commenter
  4. Blogger
  5. Moderator
  6. Supermoderator
  7. Developer
  8. Administrator

If you are Banned, you only have viewing rights in the blog which means you will not be able to do anything other than go to the homepage of view your own profile. If you are a Visitor it is basically the same as Banned, but the status looks better. Visitor accounts are for special guests such as universities if they wish to view the blog. Commenters can view and comment; Bloggers can view, comment and blog; and Moderators and any rank above that have access to the Moderator Panel on top of everything. An Admin Panel does not exist because in this blog, content is edited directly due to certain technological restrictions of the hosting platform I am using.

Promotions of ranks will very rarely, if never, happen. By default, all Year 12s are Commenters, Year 13s are Bloggers and Ms. Martin and Ms. Hills are Supermoderators. A promotion will only occur if (1) Ms. Hills or Ms. Martin says so or (2) a Year 12 becomes a Year 13 in the next school year. Demotions are also very rare. However, you must be careful not to spam the Service provided by this blog and follow the Terms of Service rigorously; otherwise, the Moderators and Supermoderators of this blog will report you and you will be Banned for a week (or any other sensible time duration).

Moving on to the commenting and blogging system, due to technological restrictions, this website cannot save comments and/or blog posts directly; therefore, I have created a workaround. It basically works like this: you go to the "Request Comment" or "Request Post" page, and you will see a form that you can fill in. Follow the instructions and fill the form in, then click "Submit" (or equivalent) and it should open up your Mail with a pre-typed email message. DO NOT ALTER THAT MESSAGE. All the pre-filled information is crucial in recognising which account made the request to comment or post. I will then read the email and if I approve it, I will add the comment in your (account) name.

For now, some features of the blog are still a bit bugged - for example, the "Menu" bar does not display 100% correctly - but it should not affect the functionality much. However, unfortunately, there is no "Report a Bug" page at the time of writing so feel free to email directly to kellett6formblog@gmail.com to report bugs. One important thing though - remember to name the subject of your email Kellett Sixth Form University Blog - Bug Report. Thank you for your cooperation!

I will be creating a post on the Terms of Service soon. Before this post comes out, please just use common sense while using the Service (i.e. no rude emails, no swearing, etc.). Thank you very much and I am looking forward to your posts and comments!
